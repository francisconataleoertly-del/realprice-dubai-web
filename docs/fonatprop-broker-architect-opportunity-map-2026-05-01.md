# FonatProp: Broker + Architect Opportunity Map

Date: 2026-05-01

Purpose: identify what FonatProp still lacks, what brokers and architects actually care about, what already exists in Dubai and France, and which service ideas could make the product harder to copy.

## Executive view

FonatProp already has the right strategic base:

- Dubai private AI valuation workflow
- public lead-capture widget
- France valuation, investment and renovation surfaces
- premium presentation

What is still missing is not another valuation box.

The strongest next move is to turn FonatProp from `valuation tool` into `decision system`:

- `Should I sign this mandate?`
- `At what price should I list it?`
- `What should I renovate first?`
- `Can I legally do the works I am imagining?`
- `What rent can I really underwrite?`
- `How much value can I create after works?`

That is the gap between a nice demo and a tool that a brokerage or architect keeps open every day.

## What a broker wants

A broker does not mainly want a dashboard.

They want:

- a faster and more defensible valuation
- a way to win seller trust
- a way to convert website traffic into real conversations
- a printable or shareable proof pack
- a pricing recommendation that is easier to defend than “this is my opinion”
- a follow-up system after the property is published

The strongest pain points still visible in the current product:

### 1. No seller-grade report layer

Today FonatProp explains valuation and shows premium surfaces, but it still needs a better `deliverable`.

The agent needs:

- a shareable valuation report
- confidence explanation
- comparable proof
- price recommendation band
- next-action language for the seller

Why this matters:

- Property Monitor explicitly offers co-branded seller, buyer and investor reports, plus instant CMAs and yield tools.
  Source: https://propertymonitor.com/products-and-services/pm/pmiq
- MeilleursAgents Pro positions its inventory tool as a reference tool to convince sellers of the fair price.
  Source: https://pro.meilleursagents.com/inventory/

### 2. No live listing health loop after publication

FonatProp is strong at pre-listing logic, but it needs a post-listing control layer:

- is the asking price above the AI range?
- how far is it from rental reality?
- how many days should this listing reasonably stay live?
- when should we cut price?
- is the lead quality weak because of price, media, or positioning?

This is where the radar can become useful operationally, not just visually.

### 3. No mandate-winning script

The valuation result should turn into:

- `suggested listing price`
- `defensible negotiation floor`
- `price to list fast`
- `price to list high`
- `renovate before listing? yes/no`

Without that, the broker still has to do the human packaging alone.

## What an architect or renovation advisor wants

Architects and renovation-minded professionals do not care only about the estimated sale value.

They want:

- whether a transformation is feasible
- whether zoning or urban planning allows it
- what risks the building and parcel carry
- what energy problems must be fixed first
- rough works cost by package
- subsidy or contractor context
- likely resale or rent uplift after works

Current France product gap:

- the renovation section is much better than before, but still leans on category ranges and material anchors
- it does not yet answer `can I really do this project here?`

That is where architecture-grade intelligence starts.

## What FonatProp still lacks

These are the highest-value gaps I see right now.

### A. Seller-facing deliverables

Needed:

- beautiful PDF report
- shareable seller link
- confidence and comparables page
- clear price strategy
- “what to do next” for the owner

This is urgent for Dubai and later useful in France.

### B. Permit-aware renovation intelligence

Needed:

- parcel + urbanism + building + risk + DPE + aid + works
- answer to `can I do this?`
- answer to `what is the cheapest viable upgrade path?`

This is where architects, developers and serious investors start paying attention.

### C. Post-renovation upside engine

Needed:

- current value
- renovation cost band
- legal/risk blockers
- expected post-renovation sale value
- expected post-renovation rent
- IRR or payback signal

That would turn renovation from “interesting information” into “investment underwriting”.

### D. Listing publication control tower

Needed:

- once published, track if the listing is green / amber / red versus model
- time-on-market expectations
- price cut suggestion
- listing quality notes
- seller follow-up reminders

This feels very sellable to agencies.

### E. Architecture-grade transformation scenarios

Needed:

- split apartment?
- create second bathroom?
- add pool?
- raise one floor?
- convert garage?
- turn a weak DPE into rentable stock?

This is much closer to what an architect or renovation adviser actually needs.

### F. Installed renovation intelligence

Needed:

- separate `materials only` from `supply + install` and `full project`
- room-level packs for bathroom, kitchen, floors, walls, windows and garage
- heavy-works bands for pool, upper-floor creation, structural changes and MEP
- net budget after aid, not only gross capex
- contractor and adviser routing once the user accepts a scenario

This is especially important in France.

Retail anchors from Leroy Merlin, Castorama, Brico Depot, IKEA and project-price guides are already useful, but they do not yet form a professional budgeting workflow on their own.

An investor or architect wants to know:

- what the bathroom package costs in basic, mid and premium quality
- what part is materials versus labour
- what gets subsidy support
- what the likely resale or rental uplift is after the work

That is where renovation stops being decorative content and starts becoming investment infrastructure.

## The most interesting service idea

## `FonatProp Project Twin`

This is the best “not just another AVM” service idea I see.

What it would do:

1. user enters address
2. FonatProp resolves building / parcel / zoning / risks / energy / rent rules
3. user selects an objective:
   - sell now
   - renovate then sell
   - buy to let
   - improve DPE
   - split / extend / reconfigure
4. system returns:
   - current value band
   - legal and technical blockers
   - works packages
   - cost bands
   - post-works value band
   - post-works rent band
   - recommended contractor / adviser next step

Why this is interesting:

- brokers can use it to win mandates
- investors can use it to underwrite
- architects can use it as a pre-feasibility layer
- sellers understand it immediately

Why I think this is still open enough:

- France has tools for valuation, DPE, alerts, urbanism, permits and rent data
- Dubai has tools for valuation, transactions, rental indices, service charges and area insights
- but the combined `valuation + legal feasibility + renovation + value uplift + next step` workflow is still not common as one premium experience

This is partly an inference from the current market.
Competitors clearly cover slices of the workflow, but the combined project-level decision layer remains fragmented.

## Another service idea with real sales value

## `FonatProp Scope Builder`

This is the most practical add-on to `Project Twin`.

What it would do:

1. user picks a project type:
   - bathroom refresh
   - kitchen replacement
   - DPE upgrade
   - rental-ready refresh
   - pool renovation
   - garage conversion
   - upper-floor creation
2. user picks the quality band:
   - basic
   - mid
   - premium
   - luxury
3. system returns:
   - materials budget
   - installed budget
   - full-project budget
   - likely subsidies
   - likely timeline
   - value uplift range
   - next adviser or contractor step

Why this is interesting:

- brokers can use it to persuade an owner before listing
- architects can use it to pre-qualify a project
- investors can use it to avoid underestimating capex
- it turns your existing renovation data into an actual workflow instead of a content block

This is also one of the clearest places where FonatProp can feel more useful than a portal or AVM product.

## If I were an agent, what would make me pay?

In order:

1. `Mandate Pack`
   - precise valuation range
   - comparable proof
   - suggested listing price
   - renovation-before-sale recommendation
   - shareable PDF

2. `Widget + lead triage`
   - capture owner data first
   - broad estimate second
   - urgency / seriousness score

3. `Listing Radar`
   - my published listings against market and model
   - price risk and cut timing

4. `France investor mode`
   - real rent
   - rent cap
   - DPE risk
   - works cost
   - aid context

## If I were an architect, what would make me care?

In order:

1. `Can this project be done here?`
2. `What blocks it?`
3. `What does the DPE force us to do first?`
4. `What contractor or adviser network is available?`
5. `What is the likely value uplift after the intervention?`

That means the architecture-facing version of FonatProp should not start from “valuation”.
It should start from `project feasibility`.

## Hard truth

If FonatProp stays only at:

- valuation
- map
- radar
- renovation ranges

it will look premium, but the market will slowly compare it to:

- Bayut / Property Finder valuation tools in Dubai
- Property Monitor or DXB Interact for professionals in Dubai
- ImmoSignal / MeilleursAgents / Yanport in France

That is dangerous.

The safer moat is:

- vertical workflow depth
- report quality
- project feasibility logic
- renovation and capex realism
- market-specific compliance intelligence

That is much harder to copy than “an AVM screen”.
